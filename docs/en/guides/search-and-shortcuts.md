# Search & shortcuts

The fastest way to get anywhere in Initiative isn't clicking — it's searching. This page covers the search box and the handful of keystrokes worth knowing.

## Search (the Command Center)

Press ++cmd+k++ (Mac) or ++ctrl+k++ (Windows and Linux) anywhere to open **search**. On a phone, a **three-finger tap** opens it too.

Start typing and Initiative looks across your current guild for:

- **Projects, tasks, and documents** by name.
- **Queues** and **counter groups**.
- **Pages** like My Tasks, My Calendar, and your settings.
- **Quick actions**, like "Add task" or "Add document."

Use the arrow keys to move through results and ++enter++ to jump. It's usually quicker than navigating the sidebar — worth making a habit.

## The recent-items tab bar

As you open projects, tasks, and documents, they stack up as **tabs** across the top, like a browser. Click a tab to return to it; close one when you're done. You can choose how many recent items it keeps (from 1 to 100) in **User settings → Interface**.

## Handy keystrokes

| Keys | What it does |
|---|---|
| ++cmd+k++ / ++ctrl+k++ | Open search |
| ++"Esc"++ | Close the search box or a dialog |
| ++shift++ + click a column | Add a column to the sort (Table view) |

There's a fuller list on the [keyboard shortcuts](../reference/keyboard-shortcuts.md) reference page.

!!! tip "When in doubt, search"
    Can't remember which initiative a document is in? Don't go looking — press ++cmd+k++ / ++ctrl+k++, type a few letters of its name, and go straight there.
